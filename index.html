Ancient Uses.

Ginkgo has been a part of traditional Chinese medicine for centuries. The leaves were used to treat brain and circulatory problems and respiratory conditions. Ginkgo nuts were used for cough, fever, diarrhea, toothaches, and even gonorrhea. It's now sold mostly as a supplement for memory support or brain health.

Anti-oxidant.

Ginkgo leaves contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which are both antioxidants.

Anti-Depressant.

One human study in older adults also found that ginkgo biloba extract could improve symptoms of depression and reduce levels of S100B, a marker of brain injury, when paired with an antidepressant.
